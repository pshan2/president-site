ADMINISTRATIVE COUNCIL

The Administrative Council of Emory University is made up of the university cabinet and senior leaders who report directly to cabinet officers. It serves as a forum for creative thought about the ways in which the many parts of the university are bound together intellectually, culturally, and socially as one community of tradition, vision, and aspiration.

The council meets once a semester with two purposes: (1) to inform and engage leadership across the university about developments that affect all or most of their areas; and (2) to create opportunities for dialogue about significant matters of policy, priorities, or strategy for the university. The council is a sounding board for the president and the cabinet in addressing long-term opportunities and issues.

The members of the Administrative Council are shown on the university’s organizational chart.
